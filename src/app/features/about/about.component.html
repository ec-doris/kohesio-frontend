ABOUT Kohesio.eu

The EU Cohesion Policy supports tens of thousands of projects across Europe, through funding programmes managed by national and regional authorities in collaboration with the European Commission. Programmes’ authorities are subject to a series of financial reporting and transparency obligations to gather and publish lists of the supported projects.

The Kohesio project aims to create a knowledge base for EU co-funded projects and related beneficiaries that is accessible to the public. It uses W3C open standards and open-source tools provided by Wikimedia Deutschland.

Kohesio is still in developmentand therefore it displays datasets of projects co-funded in the 2014-2020 programming period that is limited to a number of EU countries. The initial scope of the project covers three funds: European Regional Development Fund (ERDF), Cohesion Fund (CF) and European Social Fund (ESF).

More data from Member States will progressively enrich Kohesio.

Data source and limited warranty

EU Member States and local authorities publish lists of operations / projects and related beneficiaries as part of their information and communication obligations (Regulation (EU) 1303/2013, Annex XII and Regulation (EU) 2021/1060, article 49).

Kohesio aggregates and standardizes data from the lists of operations. All data published in the lists of operations fall under the responsibility of the respective Managing Authorities. The European Commission cannot accept any liability for the accuracy of content in Kohesio.

Data protection

Kohesio does not collect any personal information or store cookies. The only exception is clicking on the button "Projects near me" on the map, in which case your geolocation (latitude/longitude) will be stored in a log file for 30 days and then permanently deleted.

The beneficiaries’ names appearing on Kohesio originate from datasets published by the related Managing Authorities on their own websites. It is the responsibility of the Member States to ensure that these names are properly pseudonymised to avoid disclosing personal data, and to inform the beneficiaries of the use made of their data.

Translation

English translation of project titles and descriptions are automatically provided with eTranslation, the neural machine translation tool of the European Commission. While we strive to offer the best accuracy, some occasional translations may be suboptimal. You are encouraged to report them in order to improve the overall quality of the Kohesio knowledge base (contact: CNECT-ECDORIS; REGIO-KM@ec.europa.eu)
